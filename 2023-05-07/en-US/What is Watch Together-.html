What is Watch Together?

Watch Together is a collaborative way to watch YouTube videos (including YouTube Livestreams) with others. In Watch Together, everyone can participate in building a playlist of videos.

During Coachella (April 14-16, 2023 and April 21-23, 2023), you can use Watch Together on Discord to watch the Coachella YouTube livestream allowing you to watch live performances with your friends from anywhere in the world! Watch Together will handle the video syncing for you—no counting down to pressing play, and you and your friends can see each other’s reactions and chat while watching. 

How to use Watch Together to watch Coachella

Watch Together is available in voice channels on desktop, web browser, and mobile.  To start watching the Coachella YouTube livestream using Watch Together, follow these steps below. 

Desktop/Browser App

1. Join a voice channel in the server where you and your friends will watch the Coachella YouTube livestream.

2. Once joined, you should see the Activity Launcher (it looks like a rocket ship) in the voice channel controls. Press on the Activity Launcher icon to open the Activity Shelf." 

3. Then select "Watch Together." 

4. If you’ve never used Watch Together before, you will need to provide authorization. 

5. Select the featured Coachella YouTube livestream thumbnail. To pick one of the other Coachella streams featuring live music from multiple stages, press the “See More” button, which will pull up a playlist of all the 6 available Coachella livestreams. 

6. Invite friends to join you by right-clicking on the voice channel and selecting  “Invite to Join” in the pop-up menu if you’re on desktop or by pressing the “Invite to Activity” button when you hover on the menu bar in the lower-left corner of the Voice Channel window. To watch with you, your friends need to be in the same Voice Channel as you and join the Watch Together Activity you started. Each person joining will need to provide authorization if they’ve never used the Watch Together Activity before.

Mobile App

1. Join a voice channel in the server where you and your friends will watch the Coachella YouTube livestream.

2. Once joined, you should see the Activity Launcher (it looks like a rocket ship) in the voice channel controls at the bottom of the screen. Press on the Activity Launcher icon to open the Activity Shelf.

3. Then select "Watch Together."

4. If you’ve never used Watch Together before, you will need to provide authorization.

5. Select the featured Coachella YouTube livestream thumbnail.To pick one of the other Coachella streams featuring live music from multiple stages, press the “View more” button, which will pull up a playlist of all the 6 available Coachella livestreams.

6. To invite your friends to join the Watch Together activity, you will need to invite your friends to join the voice channel with the activity. Tap on the floating Discord icon or swipe up to open the voice channel controls and select Invite. Share the invite link to your friends to watch the Coachella YouTube livestream with you!

What Does It Mean To Be Hosting Or Sharing?

By default, the person who started Watch Together is Hosting, and they control what’s playing. In hosting mode, anyone can add videos to the playlist, but only the host can pause, scrub, skip, reorder, and delete any video.

If the host wants others to be able to control the video, they can toggle to Sharing mode. When sharing, all participants have controls over the playlist and the current video, and their actions apply to every user in the session. We recommend Hosting when in big groups and Sharing when in small groups!

If I Change The Volume, Does It Change Everyone Else's Volume?

Volume changes are always local, so if any user adjusts the volume or mutes the YouTube player, only their own audio is affected. 

Watching Multiple Livestream Channels

Coachella will be streaming 6 channels on YouTube. If you want to jump between streams, you can add them all to your playlist. The host can click within the playlist to change the stream that the group is watching. To find all of the official Coachella YouTube livestream channels, search for this playlist in Watch Together. To add them to your playlist, press the +. Participants in the session can continue adding videos to the playlist throughout the Watch Together session.

Playback Time Limits

Playback of videos is limited to 180 minutes if watching with a group or 30 minutes if watching alone. When the max playback limit is reached, playback will end. This will start a 10-minute cool-down, after which you can continue watching. This same timer applies no matter what server or voice chat you are in.

Other FAQs

Q: I’ve reached my playback limit but I want to continue watching Coachella. How do I keep watching? 

A: You can head to YouTube to watch the livestream individually until your 10 minute cool-down timer is over. Then you can continue watching the Coachella YouTube livestream using Watch Together after the timer is done. 

Q: Where can I find the schedule of the Coachella YouTube livestream? 

A: You can go here to learn more about the schedule. 

Q: If I pause the video, does it pause for everyone else?

A: When the remote is not shared, only the Host can pause the video. When the remote is shared, anyone can pause the video.

Q: How many people can be in Watch Together at one time? 

A: Voice Channels have a max capacity of 100 participants. You can create another Voice Channel to continue watching the Coachella YouTube livestream with others if you reach the max capacity. 

Q: Can I hide the Activity Launcher or remove the Activity from my server?

A: If you have the admin permission in your server, you can hide the Activity Launcher for certain roles. To do this, go to Server Settings > Roles > Permissions > Voice Channel Permissions and toggle off the "Use Activities" permission.

You can also hide the Activity Launcher for a channel category or specific voice channel by right-clicking on the voice channel/category and going to Voice Channel Permissions.

Q: Can I change this Activity to my preferred language?

A: This Activity is currently available only in English, but you can search for videos in different languages within the YouTube player.
