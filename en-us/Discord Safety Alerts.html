Discord safety alerts are part of our Teen Safety Assist initiative to help make Discord a safer and more private place for teens to hang out online. These safety alerts are default enabled for teens globally. No action is needed to turn safety alerts on for teens. 

What this article covers:

  • Safety alerts on senders
  • Safety alerts in chat
  • Future safety alerts
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Safety alerts on senders

1. When a teen receives direct messages (DMs) from a sender for the first time, Discord will detect if a safety alert should be sent to the teen for this DM. If detected, Discord will notify the teen with a safety alert encouraging them to double check if they want to reply, and will provide links to block the user or view more safety tips to safeguard themselves. 

If Discord does not detect the need for a safety alert for this DM, the teen will not receive the safety alert and will simply receive the message. 

This feature will not be available in the Message Requests and Spam folders. Only messages that go directly to DMs and the messages accepted then moved from Message Requests and Spam folders will be eligible for the safety alerts. 

2. If a teen presses on More Tips within the safety alert, they will find tips on what they can do to protect themselves and actions they can take such as muting or blocking the sender. The teen can also block the sender directly from the safety alert. If the teen dismisses the safety alert, they will see the message. 

3. If a teen decides to mute the sender, they can click on Mute from the alert detail.

4. If a teen decides to block the sender, they can click on Block from the alert detail.

5. If a teen wants to report a message from the sender, they can use the in-app reporting process to report the specific message. 

The safety alert feature is enabled by default for all teens. 

The safety alert will override a teen’s server privacy defaults within User Settings > Privacy & Safety. If a teen has Allow direct messages from server members enabled, they could still receive these safety alerts. 

Safety alerts in chat

1. When a teen user receives a message that we detect is potentially unwanted, a safety alert in chat will pop up to offer protections either to take action (i.e. block) or to read safety tips. This alert provides an additional layer of protection based on the message(s) received.

2. Teen users who select Take action will be prompted to either block the sender, report the sender or contact a third-party support provider. 

3. If the teen blocks the sender, they will see a confirmation that the sender is blocked. 

4. If the teen reports the sender, they will see a confirmation that the sender is reported. 

5. For teens in the United States, they will have the option to contact Crisis Text Line. For teens in other international English-prominent regions, they will be prompted to reach out to NoFiltr, a community that empowers young people to safely navigate online. 

6. When a teen user receives the initial safety alert in chat and selects Read safety tips, they will be directed to our top safety tips to protect themselves. 

7. If the teen user dismissed the initial safety alert in chat, they can still access the safety tools by tapping on the shield icon located in the upper right-hand corner. 

 

8. If another unwanted message is detected, the teen user will receive another safety alert in the chat, reminding them to protect themselves with the safety tools in the upper right-hand corner. 

For some messages that we detect may be unwanted, teen users will receive the safety alert in chat via a banner at the top of their screen. Teen users could receive this banner style alert or the full screen alert (shown above), depending on the context of the message. This will guide them to the same safety tools or a shortcut option to block the sender.

Safety alerts in chat are currently available globally, but will be in English. This is also enabled by default for all teen users in English-prominent regions. 

Teen users can manage these alerts in their Privacy settings. Simply go to User Settings ⚙️ > Privacy & Safety , then toggle the "Show alerts in direct messages" switch to on or off.

Future safety alerts

Discord will be launching additional safety alerts for teens and adults that focus on protecting against additional harm types. Check our Safety News Hub for the latest information. 

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: If a teen dismisses one safety alert and the same sender tries to message them again, will another safety alert pop up? 

A: A safety alert for a sender will only be sent once at the start of the conversation with that sender. Once the teen dismisses it, the same safety alert will not appear again. However, if a teen user receives a message that we detect as potentially unwanted, the safety alert in chat will pop up to offer protection if these alerts are enabled in the user settings.

Q: Do you send an alert to the sender? 

A: No, the sender will not be aware of the safety alert shown to the teen.

Q: Can adults opt into receiving these safety alerts?

A: No, this is a feature specifically designed for teen users. Adult users will not be able to opt into these safety alerts

Q: I’m an adult so why am I receiving these safety alerts? 

A: We only send these safety alerts to users who self reported as teens. Please check if you have provided the correct age for your account. If an incorrect age was provided, you can submit an age update request.

Q: Can I opt out of these safety alerts?

A: The safety alerts on senders are enabled for all self-reported teens and cannot be opted out of. If you believe we have made a mistake, such as sending unnecessary safety alerts, please let us know by pressing thumbs up or down on the safety alert.

The safety alerts in chat are also enabled by default for all self-reported teens. These can be opted out of by navigating to User Settings ⚙️ > Privacy & Safety, then toggling off the "Show alerts in direct messages".
